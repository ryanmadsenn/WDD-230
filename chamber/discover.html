Discover Tri-Cities

Columbia River

One of the Tri-Cities mosts popular attractions, the Columbia River is a great place to spend quality time with the family. Every summer thousands of Tri-Cities residents take to the river to boat, float, swim, and watch the annual hydroplane races. In the winter you will find some of the best fishing on this side of the Mississippi, with various types Salmon, trout, and many other fish available to catch. Don't forget your fishing license though!

Wineries

Tourists come from all over the world to visit the over 200 Tri-Cities wineries. From wine-tasting, to tours of breathtaking vineyards, you won't want to miss this awesome aspect of the Tri-Cities region.

Badger Mountain

The amazing hiking trails of Badger Mountain attract hundreds of Tri-Citians every day. Get some exercise and get rewarded with a beautiful view of the surrounding area when you make it to the top. You might even be able to get a glimpse of the many vineyards mentioned above. Keep an eye out for rattlesnakes!

Hanford Nuclear Site

Many consider the Hanford Nuclear Site to be the spark that created the now booming Tri-Cities region. It was originally one of the few locations nationwide that was tasked to manufacture plutonium for the nuclear bombs in World War II. The site is now used to clean up nuclear waste, host a commercial nuclear power plant, and house various centers for scientific research and development. Many Tri-Citians find employment at the site.

Toyota Center

The Toyota Center of Tri-Cities is a staple of the region. Many popular events are hosted here inluding hockey games for the local Western Hockey League (Semi-Professional) team the Tri-City Americans. You can also watch indoor football games from the Tri-Cities Fever football team, and a slough of other events that take place here.

Facts about Tri-Cities

  • Tri-Cities has an estimated population of over 300,000
  • Tri-Cities is one of the fastest growing metropolitan areas
  • Tri-Cities has ten public golf courses
  • There are more than 200 wineries within a one-hour drive
  • Tri-Cities is home to the tallest treeless mountain in the western hemisphere - Rattlesnake Mountain
  • Three rivers flow through the Tri-Cities: Yakima, Columbia and Snake Rivers
